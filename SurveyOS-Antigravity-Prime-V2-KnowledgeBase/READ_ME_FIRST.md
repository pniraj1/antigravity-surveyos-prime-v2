# 🚀 Welcome to Your SurveyOS Second Brain!

If you are new to Obsidian, don't worry! It is much simpler than it looks. Think of it like a **Pro version of Notepad** that understands how different ideas are connected.

## 1. How to Open This Vault

1.  **Launch Obsidian** from your Start Menu.
2.  If it's your first time, you will see a "Welcome" screen.
3.  Click the button that says **"Open folder as vault"**.
4.  A file picker will open. Navigate to:
    `c:\Users\Manasi\OneDrive\Desktop\Antigravity Surveyor V6 fixed\SurveyOS-Prime-V2\SurveyOS-Antigravity-Prime-V2-KnowledgeBase`
5.  Click **"Select Folder"**.

## 2. Best Way to Navigate

Once open, look at the left sidebar. You will see your files.
-   **Start here:** Click on `00_Project_Map.md`.
-   **Click Links:** Anything in brackets like `[[Dashboard]]` is a clickable link. Hover over it to see a preview, or click to open it.

## 3. The "Cool Part" (Graph View)

On the far left, look for an icon that looks like a **connected web** (or press `Ctrl + G`).
-   This shows your project's "Brain".
-   Every "dot" is a file. The lines show how they are connected.
-   This helps you see how a change in the `Storage` might affect the `Dashboard`.

## 4. Why call it a "Vault"?
In Obsidian, a "Vault" is just their word for a folder. It's not a secret safe or a cloud database; it's simply a collection of your notes stored safely on **your** computer.

## 5. What is it for?
This "Brain" is for YOU (the developer/owner). As the code gets bigger and more complex, you can use this to quickly remember how things work without digging through thousands of lines of code.
